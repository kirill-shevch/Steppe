# P4 plan: vegetation response and wind waves

## Goal

Replace the sparse P1 debug triangles with a continuous steppe surface that reads at two scales:

- nearby plants have stable, tangible geometry;
- the whole visible landscape moves as one field under the same wind.

Slow vegetation state is no longer part of P4. Water circulation, precipitation from
water-bearing clouds, soil memory, and rain-driven growth form a separate future task.
Likewise, P4 uses one grass asset throughout the steppe; biome-specific species and
geometry profiles are deferred. Current P1 fields may still vary density, height, colour,
wind coherence, and response frequency without replacing the plant mesh.

## Research decision

Unity Terrain detail instancing is optimized for large numbers of painted detail meshes, but Steppe uses a custom deterministic heightfield and streams its own chunks. Converting the world to `TerrainData` only to gain its detail renderer would make the terrain, biome, and streaming models fight the engine rather than cooperate with it.

Unity 6 exposes `Graphics.RenderMeshIndirect`, which draws many instances of one mesh from an argument buffer and permits arguments to be generated by either CPU or GPU. This is a smaller and more replaceable foundation than a custom `BatchRendererGroup`, while still avoiding a GameObject or draw call per plant: [Unity RenderMeshIndirect](https://docs.unity3d.com/6000.0/Documentation/ScriptReference/Graphics.RenderMeshIndirect.html).

The procedural grass presentation for *Ghost of Tsushima* validates individual GPU-generated blades for very large fields, while Sucker Punch's wind description is especially relevant to Steppe: a broad scrolling gust pattern plus a smaller detail texture drove grass, bushes, and trees together. This made the wind a readable world field instead of unrelated sine animation on each plant: [Procedural Grass in Ghost of Tsushima](https://gdcvault.com/play/1027033/), [Ghost of Tsushima wind and VFX](https://blog.playstation.com/?p=345372).

The P4 shader will follow the older but durable split described by Crytek: main bending moves the whole plant in the world-space wind direction, while detail bending adds smaller local motion. Vertex height and stiffness control how much each part moves: [Vegetation animation in Crysis](https://developer.nvidia.com/gpugems/gpugems3/part-iii-rendering/chapter-16-vegetation-procedural-animation-and-shading-crysis).

Open Unity grass experiments confirm that indirect rendering is practical in URP, but they are references rather than dependencies. For example, InfiniteGrass explicitly lists missing chunking and LOD while testing millions of candidate positions every frame. Steppe already owns canonical coordinates, chunk streaming, terrain height, biome fields, weather, and time, so a small purpose-built renderer is less risky than importing a second world system: [UnityURP-InfiniteGrass](https://github.com/Youssef-Afella/UnityURP-InfiniteGrass), [Unity-Grass](https://github.com/cainrademan/Unity-Grass).

## Selected representation

The renderer has no third-party runtime dependency. Its accepted source geometry and
atlas are the CC0 `03_t` tuft from Yughues/Nobiax, transformed at startup into a
wind-segmented three-tuft cluster by Steppe's own code. A small authored-in-code mesh
remains the missing-asset fallback.

- four vertical segments added to every source card for curved bending;
- an alpha-tested atlas shared by all clusters;
- normalized vertex height, stiffness, and cluster phase stored in mesh channels;
- one three-tuft cluster mesh instanced many times;
- deterministic position, rotation, width, height, tint, and phase per instance;
- indirect GPU drawing without one GameObject per plant.

Placement is now isolated in the pure `GrassCellDataBuilder`, while
`SteppeGrassRenderer` owns streaming and GPU resources. A later `VegetationProfile`
can replace the current resource constants without changing placement, ecology, or wind.

## Rendering architecture

```text
P1 canonical terrain + biome fields
                 |
                 +--> stable candidate anchors in 64 m vegetation cells
                 |               |
                 |               +--> shared blade/tuft mesh
                 |               +--> indirect near/middle instances
                 |
P3 wind + P4 static motion parameters
                 |
                 +--> near grass: bend, height, density, colour, stiffness
                 +--> far terrain: matching moving gust bands
```

### Near field

- Independent 64 m vegetation cells, not 512 m terrain chunks.
- A jittered canonical lattice produces even coverage without rows or chunk seams.
- Stable world-coordinate hashes decide presence and all variation.
- Cells stream around the observer and are built over multiple frames.
- One shared instance buffer and a small number of indirect draw commands render the visible set.
- The maximum candidate field remains stable. Growth and LOD select stable hash subsets instead of regenerating positions, preventing vegetation from boiling when density changes.

### Distance representation

The initial tuning ranges are deliberately provisional:

- 0-140 m: segmented geometry with full bend and close shading;
- 110-320 m: simpler instances and reduced density;
- 260 m to the terrain horizon: no individual geometry; the terrain material carries cover colour, directional highlights, and broad moving gust bands.

Overlapping dithered transitions must hide the rings. The final radii are profiling results, not design constants.

### Asset and shading contract

- Roots remain fixed; bend grows non-linearly towards the tip.
- Front and back are consistently lit without making every crossing plane a dark stripe.
- Root colour is darker; tips can catch backlight.
- Feather grass gets a strong silver directional response, making wind direction readable.
- Grass receives the main light and terrain shadow. Only the closest geometry may cast shadows.
- The P1 CPU triangle mesh remains as a capability fallback until the indirect path is proven.

## Canonical wind field

Wind is a shared world-space field, not an animation local to each chunk or plant.

```text
wind(position, time) = prevailing direction *
    (base strength + broad advected gust + fine advected turbulence)
```

- P3 remains the authority for direction and base speed.
- Broad noise creates coherent bands hundreds of metres wide.
- Fine noise prevents all plants inside a band from moving identically.
- Both layers use canonical world coordinates and advect with weather time, so floating-origin shifts and chunk reloads cannot reset their phase.
- The same broad value drives geometry bend and far-terrain darkness/silvering. A wave can therefore be followed from the horizon into individual stems.
- F5 pauses the phase; F6 accelerates it with the rest of the simulation.

P1 already supplies the biome controls needed by the shader:

| Biome | Broad coherence | Local motion |
|---|---|---|
| Meadow | very high, slow wide waves | soft detail |
| Feather grass | longest directional bands | moderate rustle and silver highlights |
| Dry steppe | weak broad wave | short high-frequency tremor |
| Desert steppe | almost none in plants | sparse rigid motion |

## Static data passed to shaders

P4 extends, rather than duplicates, P1 data:

- grass instance data: canonical anchor, orientation, base height/width, colour, wind coherence, motion frequency, and stable phase;
- terrain data: existing P1 ground/cover colour;
- global data: wind vector, weather-advection time, readable response time, and gust scales.

No weather or season change rebuilds terrain or instance placement.

## Implementation sequence

Current implementation status: P4.1 and P4.2 are now present in the prototype. It uses 64 m
canonical grass cells, one segmented three-card grass asset, indirect rendering, stable
distance thinning, terrain-level far detail, and the old P1 meshes as an automatic
capability fallback owned by a separate renderer. Grass, terrain, legacy fallback, and
the weather map share the measured `WorldWorkScheduler` budget. The authored mesh is
bent in the vertex shader by a canonical
two-scale P3 wind field; the same broad gust drives far-terrain bands. Ecological state
belongs to the separate future water-cycle task.

The accepted stationary renderer now uses the `03_t` mesh and alpha atlas from
Yughues/Nobiax's *Multi Stylized Grass* pack. The source tuft is 12 vertices and 6
triangles. At runtime its three cards are divided into four vertical joints and copied
into a three-tuft cluster (90 vertices, 72 triangles), allowing curved, independently
phased wind response and roughly three times the visual density without tripling CPU
ecology samples or instance buffers. The source is CC0;
only that mesh, its shared atlas, and the original license are stored under
`Assets/Steppe/Resources/Vegetation/NobiaxMultiStylizedGrass`. The earlier opaque
five-blade mesh remains a code-generated fallback if the authored resources are absent.

### P4.1 - Grass renderer vertical slice

1. Add the segmented blade mesh and indirect URP shader.
2. Add canonical 64 m vegetation cells and stable jittered placement.
3. Render the feather-grass biome first with fixed colour and no animation.
4. Add near/middle LOD thinning and the far terrain representation.
5. Keep P1 triangles behind a debug/fallback switch until the new path fills the steppe without visible rings.

Gate: a stationary feather-grass steppe reads as a dense continuous surface from ground level to horizon, and cell streaming produces no visible seams or spikes.

### P4.2 - Wind waves

Implemented: the weather system publishes wind velocity, accelerated/paused weather
time, and tunable gust scales as shader globals. Roots remain fixed, tip displacement
is height-weighted, biome wind coherence blends long waves with local turbulence, and
the far terrain samples the identical broad field.

1. Implement one canonical two-scale gust function shared by grass and terrain shaders.
2. Bend blade geometry along the P3 wind vector using height and stiffness.
3. Add biome coherence/frequency controls and silver feather-grass response.
4. Verify F5/F6, floating origin, cell reload, and direction changes.

Gate: one broad wave can be visually tracked across several chunks, and no plant sways against the reported wind direction.

### Future separate task - Water cycle and vegetation ecology

See [Future Water Cycle and Vegetation](Future_Water_Cycle_And_Vegetation.md). This work
has no P4 completion gate.

### Deferred - Biome-specific vegetation assets

1. Keep the same grass asset across all four P1 biomes for now.
2. Later evaluate meadow, dry-tussock, and desert meshes/species as their own art task.
3. Preserve density, height, colour, stiffness, and motion-frequency differences across ecotones.

### P4.3 - Wind tuning and profiling

1. Tune the shared grass response at x1, x10, and x100.
2. Tune geometry density, height, colour, stiffness, and wave scales across ecotones.
3. Profile Game view and a standalone build at ground and current 120 m debug-camera heights.
4. Tune radii and budgets; add a debug panel for active cells, candidate/visible instances, and CPU build time.

Gate: keys 1-4 retain the same grass mesh but show distinct density, scale, colour,
coherence, and motion-frequency regimes without changing renderer architecture.

## Performance budget and validation

Initial targets on the development PC at 1080p/60 fps:

- no GameObject, MeshRenderer, or material instance per blade;
- approximately 100k-300k visible geometric blades after biome and LOD thinning;
- vegetation CPU steady-state below 1 ms and streamed-cell work spread across frames;
- vegetation GPU budget initially below 4 ms;
- no single cell load creates a visible frame-time spike;
- no individual grass geometry beyond the middle range;
- indirect path requires `supportsComputeShaders` and indirect argument support; otherwise P1 fallback remains available.

Correctness tests:

- equal world coordinate and seed produce equal anchors and plant properties;
- neighboring cells do not duplicate or leave a placement seam;
- LOD uses nested stable subsets;
- wind phase survives chunk reload and floating-origin changes;
- x1 wind visibly oscillates without needing accelerated time;
- stronger wind produces greater mean bend and greater oscillation amplitude;
- time pause freezes broad gusts and local blade response;
- canonical P1 biome weights never change;
- near grass and far terrain sample the same broad gust phase.

## Deferred beyond P4

- caravan and player trampling, tracks, and spring-back;
- dust and loose seed particles in dry/desert steppe;
- snow accumulation and load;
- flowers, insects, and species succession;
- grazing and animal interaction;
- grass audio driven by the same gust field;
- GPU occlusion culling and a possible later migration to `BatchRendererGroup` if profiling requires it.
